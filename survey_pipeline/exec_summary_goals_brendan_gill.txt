Client: Brendan Gill, running for Congress in a special democratic primary election in the 11th congressional district of New Jersey.

Primary Goal: Assess Brendan Gill's viability in the NJ-11 Democratic primary special election against Tom Malinowski.

The benchmark poll, conducted December 5–9, 2025 with 769 likely Democratic primary voters, was designed to accomplish six specific things:

1. Establish the baseline landscape: Measure name recognition and favorability for all candidates, and set the initial horserace numbers.
2. Identify Gill's path to victory: Map which geographic, demographic, and attitudinal voter segments are most persuadable and where Gill can build a winning coalition — particularly in Essex County.
3. Test pro-Gill messaging: Identify which positive messages move voters most effectively toward Gill.
4. Stress-test Gill's vulnerabilities: Test three specific attacks — toxic workplace allegations, the $250K benefits controversy, and the "Trenton insider" frame — to understand their damage potential and measure how significantly they cut into his vote share.
5. Identify the best response strategy to attacks: Test two counter-messaging approaches — direct rebuttal vs. pivot without response — to determine which is more effective at recovering lost ground.
6. Test an anti-Malinowski attack: Gauge how effective negative contrast messaging around Malinowski's COVID-era stock trading scandal is at driving down his numbers.

The bottom line strategic objective: Give the Gill campaign a data-driven roadmap with roughly 50 days until Primary Day — defining where they stand, where the votes are, what to say, and how to survive getting hit.
